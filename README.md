Marketing Funnel & Conversion Performance Analysis
Future Interns - Data Science & Analytics (Task 3)

🎯 Objective
To analyze a telemarketing sales funnel, identify conversion bottlenecks, and provide data-driven recommendations to optimize campaign ROI and reallocate call center resources.

🛠️ Tech Stack & Methodology
Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn): Used for initial data cleaning, delimiter correction, and exploratory demographic analysis.

Feature Engineering: Engineered a binary converted metric and established a "funnel engagement" threshold (tracking users who stayed on the phone >60 seconds).

Power BI: Built an interactive, executive-facing dashboard utilizing custom DAX measures (calculating true percentage averages) to visualize demographic, seasonal, and historical campaign trends.

💡 Key Business Insights Discovered
The Demographic Shift: Blue-collar workers consumed the highest volume of call time but yielded the lowest return (7.7%). Conversely, Students (33.8%) and Retirees (27.1%) represent the highest-converting target demographics.

The Seasonality Trap: The highest volume of outreach occurred in May, resulting in an abysmal 7.2% conversion rate. Shifting the marketing budget and operational hours to Q1 (March) or Q4 (December) yields conversion rates nearing 50%.

Lead Prioritization: "Warm" leads from previous successful campaigns converted at 65.1%, proving that retaining and upselling existing relationships is significantly more profitable than cold-calling "nonexistent" leads (which hovered around 9%).

🚀 Actionable Recommendations
Instantly halt high-volume, low-yield cold calling in May.

Restructure call center scripts and target lists to prioritize historical successes, students, and retirees.
